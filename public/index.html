FitMatch reads the product page you're on, compares the garment against your own measurements, and tells you which size to pick — with an honest confidence score. Fewer returns, less guessing.

How it works

FitMatch uses the most reliable source available, in this order:

1 · The store's size chart

If the page lists real measurements, they're matched exactly to your body. Highest confidence.

2 · AI estimate

No chart? The AI reads the product photos, the model reference ("181 cm wears M") and your measurements to judge the fit. Shown as an estimate, never as fact.

3 · Generic chart

Nothing readable? It falls back to a standard chart — clearly marked as a rough guide only.

Built to be trusted

A size recommendation is only useful if you can rely on it.

Anchored to the model

Instead of inventing numbers, it reasons from what's known: the model's height and size, and how the garment actually drapes in the photos.

Honest about uncertainty

Every result carries a confidence score, and estimated measurements are marked with ~ so you always know what's solid and what's a best guess.

Fit per body zone

See how it sits on chest, shoulders, waist and hips — not just a single letter.

Consistent, not random

The same garment always gives the same answer, and your fit preference is applied by fixed rules — never left to chance.

Your measurements stay yours

Your body measurements are stored locally in your browser — there are no accounts, no sign-up, and nothing is sold or shared with advertisers.

To analyse a page, the product's text and image links are sent to our server for the AI assessment. Read the full privacy policy.

Affiliate disclosure

FitMatch may earn a commission if you choose to click a link to a retailer from the extension and go on to buy something. This never affects the size we recommend, and it costs you nothing. Links that can earn a commission are labelled as such.
